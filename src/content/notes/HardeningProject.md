The Hardening Project is a 3-day cybersecurity event held annually at various locations across Japan. Teams made up of employees from different companies collaborate to defend a system consisting of multiple servers, including DNS, CMS, reverse proxy, and database servers, running on both Windows and Linux. Over the course of 8 hours each day, teams work to protect their systems from a group of attackers.

This year, I had the pleasure of participating (thank you to Eyes Japan for sponsoring me!) and traveling to Okinawa, both of which were firsts for me. Read more in my article to hear my thoughts and reflections on the experience.
